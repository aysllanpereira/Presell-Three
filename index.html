1. Harvard conducts key research on obesity epidemiology, focusing on preventing and controlling obesity, especially starting in childhood. They explore how social and behavioral environments influence weight gain and advocate for lifestyle interventions like low glycemic index diets, which limit processed carbohydrates. These diets have shown promise in promoting long-term weight loss. Harvard also emphasizes early prevention to stop childhood obesity from persisting into adulthood(Harvard School of Public Health Harvard Gazette).

2. Johns Hopkins focuses on understanding metabolic processes like bioenergetics and endocrine regulation that govern metabolism and obesity. They study how the body processes nutrients and how hormonal regulation affects energy balance, exploring interventions to regulate metabolism and prevent weight gain(Home).

3. The NIH leads extensive research on obesity, focusing on how biological and environmental factors contribute to metabolic diseases like obesity and diabetes. NIH research highlights the health disparities related to obesity, with certain populations, such as African Americans and Hispanics, being at higher risk. NIH also conducts clinical trials to test interventions and better understand the causes of obesity(Obesity Research NIH)(NHLBI, NIH).

4. Mayo Clinic focuses on addressing the physiological causes of obesity through collaborative research and clinical treatment. Their initiatives aim to understand the cellular and molecular basis of fat storage and long-term health risks. Their goal is to develop more effective treatments and potentially prevent obesity(Mayo Clinic).

5. NIDDK (National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases): The NIDDK is deeply involved in obesity research, studying the causes, consequences, and treatments of obesity. They investigate how factors like genetics, the microbiome, and metabolic pathways influence body weight regulation. NIDDK also runs several landmark studies, such as the Look AHEAD trial, which focuses on long-term lifestyle changes for weight loss and diabetes prevention(NIDDK)(Obesity Research NIH).
